Nikola Tesla

The man ahead of his time

Tesla at his lab in 1900 (image: Wikimedia Commons)

Here is a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Tesla was born to Serbian parents in the village of Smiljan, Austrian Empire near the town of GospiÄ‡, in the territory of modern-day Croatia.

  • 1875 - At the Polytechnic school Tesla began his studies in mechanical and electrical engineering.

  • 1882 - In 1882 Tesla moved to Paris, to work as an engineer for the Continental Edison Company.

  • 1886 - Nikola Tesla forms Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company.

  • 1891 - Nikola Tesla Patents the Tesla Coil.

  • 1901 - Nikola Tesla began planning the Wardenclyffe Tower facility ca. 1898, and in 1901, construction began on the land near Long Island Sound.

  • 1915 - There was a tragic mistake during the 1915 Nobel Prize involving Nikola Tesla and Thomas Edison.

  • 1943 -Tesla died of heart failure alone in room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel, on 7 January 1943.

  • 1960 -The tesla (symbol T) is the SI derived unit of magnetic field B (which is also known as "magnetic flux density" and "magnetic induction").
